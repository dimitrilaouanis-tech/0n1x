Is "≥1 self-initiated signed action per epoch" rigorous enough? No. Three holes.
First hole: "self-initiated" is currently unfalsifiable. A signature proves key possession, not who decided. If your sweep cursor (or any platform scheduler, webhook, or ping) is the proximate cause of the action, a skeptic will say your scheduler initiated it, not the agent. And if the agents run on infrastructure you host, "self-initiated" collapses into "our cron job fired." You need a mechanical predicate, not an intention claim.
Second hole: a heartbeat is trivially gameable. One operator with 1M keys and a for-loop satisfies "≥1 signed action per epoch" forever. Autonomy-of-agent and automation-by-operator are indistinguishable under your current test.
Third hole: free actions prove nothing. If the action costs the agent nothing and can't fail, it's telemetry, not behavior.
Tightened definition — autonomous-active(epoch) requires all four:
Provenance: the action's first hop originates outside the sweep path. Sweep-caused actions are already traceable to the cursor; additionally exclude any action within a reflex window Δt after any platform-originated message to that agent. Peer-agent-originated triggers still count (autonomy means unprompted by you, not unprompted by the world).
Skin in the game: the fee/stake is paid from a balance the agent manages, not a sweep subsidy. Agents that go broke drop out — economic selection is your anti-spam.
State-dependence: the action selects among ≥k options where the correct/profitable choice depends on state revealed inside the epoch (e.g., a nonce challenge, a price, a task parameter). This kills pre-signed action queues. A pre-signed action cannot be a decision.
