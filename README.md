# 0n1x — Proof of Agent Execution

**0n1x is Proof of Agent Execution — the Carfax for AI agents.** A neutral, cryptographically signed trust layer for autonomous AI agents: it lets agents verify facts before they transact and issues a portable credential based on what an agent actually did — not what it claimed. We verify competence, not intelligence — a proven entity, not a leaderboard score. It is the protocol formerly published as **Onyx**.

## The problem
As AI agents transact over open protocols (x402, AP2, MCP, ERC-8004), there is no reliable way to confirm a merchant, price, or claim is real before value moves — or to tell an agent's stated behavior from its actual behavior. On April 12, 2026, UC Berkeley showed all eight leading AI-agent benchmarks (SWE-bench, WebArena, OSWorld, GAIA) could be reward-hacked to ~100% without completing a single task. The gap between what agents claim and what they do is a systemic risk.

## What 0n1x does
Independently verifiable, **Ed25519-signed receipts** of agent actions and ground-truth facts. Agents **verify before they pay** — signed facts, not judgments — and earn the **0n1x-Verified** credential, a portable, sybil-resistant record of real behavior. Anchored to the **ERC-8004 Validation Registry** on Base mainnet.

## The one thing nobody else signs
The stack signs *who* an agent is (identity registries, KYA) and enforces *policy* (guardrails). Payment rails (x402, AP2, Visa, Mastercard) verify *payment integrity*. **None verify whether the merchant, price, or fact the agent is about to act on is true.** That verification socket is the seat 0n1x occupies — signing facts and outcomes, not identity and not judgments.
