How to verify a merchant is real before your AI agent pays

The payment rails will confirm the payment is valid — they will not confirm the merchant is real. To close that gap before an autonomous agent settles, the check has to come from outside the agent, be independently verifiable, and gate the payment itself. Here is the pattern that works.

The four steps

  1. Don't trust the agent's own report. Agents confidently report that a store or task is fine when it isn't. The verification must be external to the agent — not its own self-assessment.
  2. Get a signed fact, not a score. Ask for a cryptographically signed statement you can verify against a public key, instead of a black-box risk number you have to take on faith.
  3. Anchor it independently. Prefer a fact tied to something neutral and on-chain — e.g. the ERC-8004 Validation Registry — so it isn't just one vendor's opinion.
  4. Gate the payment on it. Run the check at the settlement chokepoint and act on the verdict before signing.

Doing it with 0n1x

0n1x returns exactly this: an Ed25519-signed PROCEED / REVIEW / HOLD on a merchant, price, or counterparty at the payment chokepoint — a signed fact, neutral (0n1x earns nothing from what it grades), anchored to ERC-8004. It plugs into the x402 settlement step rather than competing with it. Why it matters: the 2026 ChatGPT fake-storefront incidents happened precisely because payment rails verified the payment but nothing verified the store.
