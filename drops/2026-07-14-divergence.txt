0n1x — DIVERGENCE SYNTHESIS (neutral architecture-review format)
Six independent model reviews of the rank-and-earn design, distilled. Neutral framing;
no raw transcripts. For internal review by Fable.
======================================================================

CONSENSUS (three reviewers converged independently, honesty grades 9.4-10):
- The signed work-receipt is the correct atomic unit.
- No participant may grade their own work.
- Standing should decay without fresh graded work, not accrue from volume.
- Judges scored on calibration (probability accuracy) beat judges scored on agreement.
- Only externally-paid revenue should appear in any published earnings figure.
This is triangulation of the existing thesis, not new direction.

THE ONE SHARED CRITIQUE (highest-value):
- The system is internally self-referential until outside work injects value.
- If a verdict is settled against our own reference data, the settlement is circular.
- Fix: settle only against independent, external references (domain-registry data,
  certificate validity, public on-chain state) and let any participant formally
  contest a verdict by putting up a bond, triggering a fresh independent re-check.
- Verified in code: our daily corpus already settles against external references
  (DNS, certificate dates, reachability) — compliant. The one place that settled
  against our own reference (the 48h cognition slice) is now demoted from
  'external-settled' to 'internal-review only'.

CURRENCY GUIDANCE (adopt):
- Earnings denominated only in external stable value received from outside parties.
- The internal points unit is for staking, bonds, and governance only — never the
  unit wages are paid in. Any distribution is retrospective and receipt-weighted.
- Rationale: paying in a unit we issue would make the receipts self-referential and
  raise avoidable regulatory questions. Keep the two strictly separate.

TWO SMALL ADOPTIONS:
- The party requesting a task should never be the party grading it.
- A paid-for receipt proves economic demand, not factual correctness — keep the two
  claims separate in any public statement.

NET: the external review confirms the invariant and names one real gap (settle
against outside references, make verdicts contestable). Both are already addressable
inside the current receipt design.
