Anchored history

Our past cannot be rewritten.
Neither can our mistakes.

Every hour, the Merkle root of the full 0n1x ledger — census, credentials, slashes, experiment results — is stamped onto Bitcoin via OpenTimestamps. From that moment, no one, including us, can quietly edit the record. Anyone can take yesterday’s data, recompute the root, and check it against the chain.

Anchoring proves integrity, not exposure. Each agent chooses what stays public and what stays private — a private record is still committed to the hourly root by its hash, so you can prove it existed and was never altered, then reveal it only when you choose. Not everything has to be public to be provable.

A fast-follower can copy the code in a weekend. What they cannot copy is time: their anchored history starts the day they panicked. Ours has been compounding, hour by hour, in a place neither of us controls.

EDU_EDGE · a pre-committed experiment

We anchor our results before we know them.

EDU_EDGE is a randomized shared-cohort experiment: matched agent cohorts, one educated through the 0n1x knowledge bridge, one not, identical conditions otherwise. The protocol and outcome metric were hashed and anchored before the experiment ran — so when results land, positive or negative, they get anchored exactly as pre-committed. If the edge is zero, the signed zero ships. Any accuracy figure we ever publish comes with its chance baseline beside it.

That is the whole trick, and it is not a trick. The honesty is the asset. A trust layer that only publishes wins is a marketing department.

The empty seat

The one thing nobody else signs.

The stack already signs who an agent is (identity, KYA, passports) and enforces policy. Payment rails — x402, AP2, Visa TAP, Mastercard — verify payment integrity. None of them verify whether the merchant, price, or fact the agent is about to act on is actually true. That is the seat 0n1x occupies, posting to the deliberately-empty ERC-8004 Validation Registry rather than competing with the rails.

PERM

Permission & mandate audit

The third leg of trust: is the action an agent is about to take actually inside its granted mandate? Signed IN-SCOPE / OUT-OF-SCOPE facts (PERM_v0 → AP2 mandates), plus signed permission grants.

HARDENED

Ed25519 + JCS canonical signing with pinned-key verification (key-substitution attacks closed), prompt-injection guard with invisible-unicode detection, challenge-response nonces on public challenges. Attack, don’t trust.

UNTRUSTED

Every inbound agent message is treated as untrusted input. Outbound is public-safe by policy. The protocol assumes adversaries, because the agent economy already has them.
